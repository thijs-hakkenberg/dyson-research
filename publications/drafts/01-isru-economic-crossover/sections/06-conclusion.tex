\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%% ============================================================

Given the assumed priors and model structure (Table~\ref{tab:canonical}), 94.7\% of \emph{converging} parameter draws place a 20{,}000-unit program within the ISRU savings window at $r = 5\%$ (95\% bootstrap CI: [94.2\%, 95.1\%]); the corresponding \emph{unconditional} probability over all 10{,}000 MC draws is ${\sim}$80.6\% (Table~\ref{tab:savings_survival}). Crossover is achieved in 85.1\% of draws (conditional median ${\sim}$4{,}300; KM median ${\sim}$5{,}350; 95\% CI: [4{,}198, 4{,}423]). Of converging runs, 22\% are analytically permanent; the remaining 78\% show no re-crossing within the searched horizon $N = 200{,}000$ (the savings-window characterization is therefore a lower bound; an extended-bound subset characterization is planned for the next revision). However, these probabilities are dominated by ISRU capital $K$ (63\% of variance), which rests on order-of-magnitude estimates: at $K$ median \$65B, crossover probability is 85\%; at \$100B, it drops to 67\%; at \$150B, to 46\% (Table~\ref{tab:k_median_sweep}). The headline results are therefore best read as a conditional surface over $K$, not as unconditional predictions. Model-form sensitivity (Table~\ref{tab:logistic_comparison}, Eq.~\ref{eq:logistic_saturation}) confirms qualitative robustness: alternative learning saturation forms shift the crossover by $\pm$1{,}500 units without eliminating it; the logistic form and yield parameter ($Y$) are tested deterministically only. Three factors prevent crossover: vitamin costs $> {\sim}\$50{,}000$/kg, discount rates $> {\sim}20\%$, or $p_s \lesssim 70\%$. For revenue-generating infrastructure, deployment delay offsets cost savings above ${\sim}\$0.94$M/unit/yr---a finding particularly consequential for space solar power. The ISRU advantage is strongest for non-revenue infrastructure. A hybrid strategy---Earth-first, switching to ISRU at $N^*$---is value-positive at program scales $\geq 20{,}000$ units.
