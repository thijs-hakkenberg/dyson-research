The headline finding · raised independently by Claude and GPT-5.5

The 95% savings-window claim is conditional, not unconditional

Both critical reviewers identified the same load-bearing problem in the abstract and conclusion — independently and without prompting.

The claim

Abstract: "95% of draws place a 20,000-unit program within the ISRU savings window." The conclusion repeats this. Reads as a probability over all Monte Carlo draws.

What the table actually shows

Table 14 reports 94.7% [94.2%, 95.1%] — and that's conditional on converging runs only. Only 85.1% of all runs converge at r=5%. The unconditional probability is ≈ 80.6%, not 95%.

Why it matters

This is a headline result. Reporting a conditional probability as if it applies to all parameter draws materially overstates the robustness of the ISRU case. Both critical reviewers explicitly require this be corrected before publication.

Companion issue

The N** re-crossing analysis is censored at the search bound (200,000 units): the median N** equals the bound itself, with IQR [200000, 200000]. The "78% functionally permanent" claim therefore states only that re-crossing doesn't occur within 200,000 units — not where it actually occurs. Claude

Distinctive findings

What each reviewer uniquely caught

Claude Opus 4.7

Asymmetric treatment + structural critique

Logistic vs piecewise plateau asymmetry: piecewise plateau is stochastically integrated; logistic saturation is only deterministic at three points. The headline 85% convergence rate is therefore not model-form-robust.

ps failure model double-counts low-A / high-Cops scenarios already in the MC.

Length / table proliferation: ~50 pages, >20 tables. Permanent/transient framework scattered across §3.2.3, §4.4, §4.6 — should be consolidated.

Gemini 3.1 Pro

Most positive — minor remaining asks

Block-deployment revenue dynamics: for SSP-style architectures where revenue starts only when a full array is complete, the continuous breakeven equation overstates delay penalty. Author defers to future work — Gemini asks for a 2–3 sentence heuristic at minimum.

Logistic vs piecewise: briefly state why the piecewise was chosen (computational? parameter elicitation?).

Otherwise: praises the decision tree, vitamin BOM table transparency, and AI disclosure as exemplary.

GPT-5.5 Pro

Found math errors and framing problems

Numerical inconsistencies in launch-learning examples: at n=10,000 with LRL=0.90, the learnable component should be ≈ $200/kg, not the stated ≈ $334/kg. Asks for a unit-test audit.

NPV not service-equivalent: ISRU delivers later, so its costs are discounted more — biases cost-only NPV toward the slower pathway. Need a common-deadline or shadow-service-value sensitivity.

Asymptotic floor thresholds inconsistent with stated equations under launch-learning. PRCC for t0 is interpreted backwards in the text.

Pre-resubmission punch list

What to fix before resubmitting

Synthesized priority order. Most can be done without re-running the full MC ensemble; only #1, #4, and #6 require additional simulation or model changes.

1
Recompute and relabel all savings-window probabilities as conditional vs. unconditional. Make the unconditional (~80.6%) the primary abstract/conclusion claim. Soften "functionally permanent" → "no re-crossing within the search horizon N=200,000"; run a 500-sample subset at N=10⁶ to estimate the true tail.
2
Add a service-equivalent NPV sensitivity — common-deadline, optimally-delayed Earth, or a shadow-service-value term — so the cost-only NPV isn't biased toward the slower pathway.
3
Audit the learning-curve numerical examples against the equations and code. Add unit tests for analytical values of n^b at the stated learning rates. Re-derive all asymptotic floor thresholds under each active configuration.
4
Stochastically integrate the logistic saturation (or one alternative model form) and report a joint convergence rate. Without this, the headline 85% can't be defended as model-form-robust.
5
Promote n0 × LRE to the main text. Justify plausible values for the modeled program class. Consider stochastic integration of n0 ~ Uniform[0, 300].
6
Either include recurring maintenance/spares in the canonical MC, or anchor K against at least one terrestrial heavy-industry reference (offshore platform, refinery, modular nuclear) and report whether the $65B median is consistent.
7
Decompose ps into catastrophic vs. degraded-operations components so the binary psmin threshold doesn't double-count low-A scenarios already in the MC.
8
Reconcile vitamin BOM: distinguish 15% Earth-sourced at early maturity from 5% modeled irreducible fraction. State explicitly where coatings, seals, sensors, and wiring costs enter the model.
9
Add uncertainty annotations to the decision tree (or move it to the appendix and provide a simplified probabilistic chart in the main text).
10
Resolve the code commit hash, freeze a release with DOI, archive requirements + seed + reproduction command. Tighten the few residual "validate" phrasings.
Bottom line

Real progress, but the headline number doesn't yet survive scrutiny.

Version AM is a substantively improved manuscript — Kaplan–Meier, phased capital, dynamic vitamin, stochastic plateau, savings-window framing, decision tree are all genuine forward motion. Two of three reviewers nonetheless flag Major Revision, primarily because the abstract's 95% savings-window claim is a conditional probability reported as if unconditional, and several supporting analyses (re-crossing, ps, model-form alternatives, learning-curve numerical examples) have asymmetries or inconsistencies that the same critical reviewers can identify independently.

None of these are existential. The fixes cluster around honest probability labeling, model-form symmetry, numerical audit, and code archival. Most can ship in the next revision without re-running the full MC.
